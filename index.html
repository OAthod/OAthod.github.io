What is OAthod?

  • OAthod starts on May 9th, 2014, in South Korea. It's name was originally "OCRG", "Open Computing Research Group", meant to be the open community for research in variety of computing technology. The number of the original members back then were thirteen, and they most were the students of Dongsung High School, which places in Jongro, Seoul.

  • OCRG was designed for researches related to the computing on wide field, the specialists in other major always take parts in it, like artists or mathematicians. And we are operating offline, and online regular computing and various (group) studies for the members and associates.

  • June 17th, 2014, OCRG has been renamed to "OAthod", meaning "Oathing Methods". Because the original name, "OCRG", was quite difficult for pronouncing, and also not impressive enough for the people.

(6/17/2014) OAthod currently has thirteen members, Facebook group, and Kakao group(only for the Korean). The current major project is "Seiland"(http://oathod.github.io/Seiland).

Is OAthod the organization for open-source projects?

  • No. This page and linked projects in Github is meant to be open-source, but the other projects not listed below are closed-source, and they may be for the commercial purpose and profit, under the closed team environment.

By the way, the first name of this team, OCRG, were meant that there's no restriction on the associates, who has been recruited and volunteered freely.
